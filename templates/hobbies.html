Some of the books that I read
Trevor Noah’s Born A Crime

He talks about The apartheid regime, how colonialists convinced different tribes to turn on each other and also talks about his existence, which was actually illegal because the regime outlawed relationships between people of different races, and he was mixed. He continues to talk about how he struggled to realize himself in a world where he wasn’t even supposed to exist, which relates to the title of the book “born a crime.”

Educated by Tara Westover

In this memoir, Tara talks about how she didn’t exist: She had no birth certificate, never attended school, and has never been to any hospital due to her father. At sixteen, Tara left home and found out about the transformative power of education, and the price she had to pay for it.
